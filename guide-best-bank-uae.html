Best bank in UAE: how to choose without guessing

The best bank is not the same for everyone. Start with how you get paid, how you spend, how often you transfer money and how much balance you keep.

MOVO Guides · Updated 23 June 2026

Needs first

What to compare first

  1. Salary and minimum-balance rules.
  2. Everyday card and ATM costs.
  3. Savings options for idle cash.
  4. International transfer fees and rates.

Start with your use case

If your salary arrives every month and you mainly spend locally, a strong salary account may matter more than the highest savings headline. If you keep a larger balance, minimum-balance rules and savings rates become more important. If you support family abroad, transfer costs can matter more than the card design.

A simple way to choose is to score each bank against the jobs you actually need: salary, card spending, savings, transfers, app quality and support. Do not let one welcome offer hide a poor fit elsewhere.

Check the costs you will actually pay

Look for monthly fees, fall-below-balance fees, ATM charges, card replacement fees, transfer charges and foreign-currency markup. A bank can be cheap for one customer and expensive for another, depending on salary, balance and travel.

Useful shortcut.Open the bank's Key Facts Statement and Schedule of Charges before applying. Search for "minimum balance", "salary transfer", "international transfer" and "foreign currency".

Use MOVO as the shortlist

Use the bank pages to shortlist options, then check the latest terms on the bank's official website before you apply. If two banks look close, choose the one that makes your most frequent task easier.

Quick decision rule

  1. Pick the account that fits your monthly routine.
  2. Do not keep avoidable fees in the setup.
  3. Separate spending money from savings.
  4. Verify final terms with the bank before applying.
