How to compare savings accounts without falling for the headline rate

“Up to 6%” is not a complete offer. Find out which part of your money earns it, for how long and what you must do to qualify.

MOVO Guides · Updated 21 June 2026

Advertised rate
up to 6.0%

What you’ll learn

  1. Why “up to” changes the calculation.
  2. Which conditions matter most.
  3. How to compare actual expected earnings.
  4. Where emergency savings should live.

Treat the headline as an invitation to read

A high number is designed to stop the scroll. It may apply only to new money, a particular balance tier, a salary plan, a limited campaign period or customers who complete several monthly actions. None of that makes the offer bad. It means the percentage alone is not enough to compare it.

Open the product page, then find the Key Facts Statement, terms and fees. Write down the answers instead of relying on memory.

Translate every “up to”.Ask: up to how much money, for which customers, until what date, and after completing which conditions?

Check the five conditions

  1. Eligible balance. Does the rate apply to the entire balance or only one tier?
  2. New money. Does only the increase above a previous balance qualify?
  3. Salary or plan. Must you transfer salary or pay a monthly subscription?
  4. Access. Can you withdraw at any time without losing the return?
  5. Campaign end. What happens when the promotional period finishes?

Also check whether the bank describes the return as interest, expected profit or another product-specific term. The calculation and certainty can differ, especially for Islamic banking products. Read the product’s own explanation.

Compare dirhams, not percentages

Estimate what you will actually earn over the time you expect to keep the money there. For a rough simple-interest estimate:

Balance × annual rate × months ÷ 12AED 50,000 at 4% for six months is roughly AED 1,000 before any applicable conditions, fees or changes in balance.

Now subtract the cost of qualifying. If a plan costs AED 49 a month, six months costs AED 294. If the account requires you to hold extra cash earning little or nothing, include that opportunity cost too. A lower, unconditional rate can produce a better real result.

Match the account to the money

Emergency money needs quick, reliable access. A goal twelve months away can tolerate more restrictions. Everyday spending should not sit in the same place as long-term savings if that makes the balance confusing.

  • Emergency fund: prioritize access, stability and no withdrawal penalty.
  • Short-term goal: compare guaranteed or clearly defined returns over the exact term.
  • Money needed this month: prioritize payment access, not yield.

Do not move an emergency fund for a tiny rate difference if the new setup makes access harder or introduces unfamiliar conditions.

Remember

  1. A headline rate is the start of the comparison.
  2. Calculate with the balance and period that apply to you.
  3. Subtract the cost of meeting the conditions.
  4. For emergency money, access matters as much as return.

Rates and product conditions change. Recheck the bank’s official product page and Key Facts Statement before opening or moving money. CBUAE standards require clear disclosure of product terms and fees: Consumer Protection Standards.
