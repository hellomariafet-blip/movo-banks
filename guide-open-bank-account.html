How to open your first bank account in the UAE

The application is often the easy part. The useful work happens before it: choosing the right account, preparing the documents and checking what “free” really means.

MOVO Guides · Updated 21 June 2026

  4. What to do as soon as the account is active.

Start with the job, not the bank

A bank account is a tool. Before comparing logos and welcome offers, decide what the tool needs to do. Most people need an account for salary, rent, bills, local transfers and a debit card. Some also need inexpensive international transfers, several currencies or a separate place for savings.

For everyday spending, a current or salary account is usually the practical starting point. A savings account is better for money you do not plan to spend this week. One account can technically do both jobs, but keeping spending and savings separate makes the balance much easier to understand.

A simple setup works.Use one account for income and bills, one card for everyday spending and one savings space for your emergency fund. Add complexity only when it solves a real problem.

Prepare the document pack

Requirements differ by bank and customer status. A UAE resident will commonly be asked for a valid passport, Emirates ID and proof of address. A salary account may also require a salary certificate, employer letter or recent payslips. The bank can request additional information to complete its identity and compliance checks.

Make clear scans before you start. Check that names, dates and document numbers are readable and consistent. A blurry passport page or an old address document can turn a ten-minute application into several days of messages.

Valid passport and Emirates ID, if applicable.

2

Read the account like a monthly bill

The headline may say “no monthly fee”, but the condition may be a minimum balance or salary transfer. If you stop meeting it, a fee can appear. Download the Key Facts Statement and Schedule of Charges before applying. Search for these six items:

  • monthly or plan fee;
  • minimum balance and fall-below fee;
  • salary-transfer condition;
  • ATM fees outside the bank’s network;
  • local and international transfer fees;
  • foreign-currency markup on card payments.
Do the boring calculation.If Account A is free only above AED 3,000 and Account B costs AED 25 a month with no balance rule, the cheaper option depends on how you actually manage money—not on the word “free”.

Set it up before using it

Once the account opens, do not immediately move every dirham into it. First confirm your name and IBAN, activate the card through the official app, set a strong passcode and enable transaction notifications. Then make a small transfer and a small card payment.

Add your own transfer limits, freeze any features you do not need and save the bank’s official support number. Never share an OTP, PIN or app approval code—even with someone claiming to work for the bank.

Remember

  1. Choose an account around your monthly routine.
  2. Prepare clean, current documents.
  3. Check the Key Facts Statement, not just the ad.
  4. Test the account with small amounts before making it primary.

Requirements can change and banks may request extra documents. Verify the current terms on the bank’s official website. Background references: CBUAE Consumer Protection Standards and Emirates NBD document guide.
