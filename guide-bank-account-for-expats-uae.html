UAE bank account for expats: what to compare first

For expats, the right account is usually the one that handles salary, rent, family transfers and travel without surprise costs.

Documents and eligibility

Requirements vary by bank and customer profile. Residents are commonly asked for identity documents, Emirates ID, address details and income information. If you are new to the UAE, prepare clear scans and keep employer details ready.

If a bank cannot verify something during onboarding, the application can slow down. Check the bank's official document list before starting.

Salary and balance rules

Some accounts are easier or cheaper when salary is paid into the bank. Others depend on a monthly average balance. For a new expat, a low-friction salary account may be more useful than a complicated premium account.

Ask one boring question."What happens if my salary is late or my balance drops below the limit?" The answer tells you whether the account fits real life.

Transfers and home-country money

If you send money abroad often, compare the full cost: fee, exchange rate, delivery speed and limits. A low fee does not always mean a low total cost if the exchange rate is worse.

Use MOVO transfers to compare routes, then verify the final price inside the provider before sending.

Good expat setup

  1. One salary account for income and bills.
  2. One savings space for emergency money.
  3. One reliable transfer route for family or home-country costs.
  4. Notifications and limits turned on from day one.
